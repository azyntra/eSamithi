eSamithi — Child Safety Standards

Effective date: 16 July 2026 · Applies to the eSamithi mobile app and member services

eSamithi (operated by Azyntra Technologies) maintains a zero-tolerance policy towards child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) in any form, anywhere on our platform. This page publishes the standards we hold ourselves and our users to, and how concerns are reported and acted on.

Scope of the service: eSamithi is a member portal for registered adult members (18+) of community welfare societies in Sri Lanka. There are no public accounts, no open chat, and no content visible outside a society's own verified membership. The only user-generated content is Puruka — a classifieds-style exchange visible solely within a member's own society.

1. Prohibited content and conduct

The following are strictly prohibited on eSamithi and result in immediate content removal and account termination:

  • Child sexual abuse material (CSAM) of any kind — real, drawn, computer-generated or implied — in any listing, photo, request or message.
  • Any content or behaviour that sexualises, exploits, endangers or facilitates harm to a minor, including grooming, sextortion, trafficking or the advertisement of any such activity.
  • Use of the app by or on behalf of anyone under 18. Enrolment requires verification against a society's member register using a national identity card number and date of birth.

2. How to report a concern

  • In the app: use the Requests feature (More → Requests) to report any listing or content to your society's officers, who can remove it immediately.
  • Directly to the platform operator: email azyntra@gmail.com with the subject "Child safety report". Reports are reviewed within 24 hours.

3. Moderation and enforcement

  • Puruka listings are visible only inside a single society and can be removed at any time by that society's authorised officers or by the platform operator.
  • On any credible CSAE report we immediately remove the content, suspend the account, and preserve evidence for law enforcement.
  • Because every account is tied to a verified society member register entry, offenders are identifiable to their society and to the authorities.

4. Compliance with law and reporting to authorities

We comply with all applicable child-safety laws, including the Penal Code of Sri Lanka and the National Child Protection Authority Act. Confirmed CSAM or exploitation is reported to the relevant authorities:

  • National Child Protection Authority (Sri Lanka) — Childline 1929 · childprotection.gov.lk
  • Sri Lanka Police — emergency 119 / Children & Women Bureau
  • Where content involves other jurisdictions, the relevant hotline or the NCMEC CyberTipline.

5. Designated point of contact

The designated child-safety contact for eSamithi, prepared to discuss our CSAE prevention practices and compliance, is the platform operator: azyntra@gmail.com (Azyntra Technologies, Sri Lanka).
