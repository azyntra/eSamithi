eSamithi — How the System Works

eSamithi is a digital platform for Sri Lankan community welfare societies (මරණාධාර හා සුභසාධක සමිති). It keeps each society's member register, contributions, loans and notices in one safe place — and puts them in every member's pocket. This document explains, in plain language, where everything runs and how the pieces fit together.

Architecture overview · Version 1.3.0 · July 2026 · Azyntra Technologies
📱

Members use the mobile app

Free on Google Play. A member sees their own contributions, loans and deposits, reads society notices with instant notifications, shows a digital membership card with a QR code, and trades within the society on Puruka — in Sinhala or English.

💻

The society office uses the desktop app

Officers manage the member register, record payments and loans, prepare reports and letters, and publish notices — exactly the books the society already keeps, minus the paperwork.

🖥️

The operator uses the admin console

The platform team brings new societies on board in minutes, watches the health of every server, and can assist a society's office when asked — with every support action recorded in an audit log.

The system at a glance

🔒 Everything encrypted (HTTPS) 🗄️ Each society gets its own locked database ☁️ 2 cloud servers — one for daily service, one as the control room ▶️ Published on Google Play (v1.3.0) 🔔 Real push notifications 🌐 Full Sinhala + English 📝 Audited operator access 🚫 No ads · no data selling · no trackers

What the software is made of

the software architecture view — three layers, plus protections built into every layer

The apps — what people see

📱 Member mobile app (Android)
  • Contributions, loans & deposits
  • Notices with push notifications
  • Digital membership card + QR
  • Puruka — society marketplace
  • Requests to the office
💻 Office desktop app
  • Member register & enrolment
  • Payments, loans, deposits, funds
  • Meetings, letters & reports
  • Publish notices & broadcasts
  • Works offline-friendly at the office
🖥️ Operator console (web)
  • Fleet dashboard — all societies
  • Onboarding wizard (minutes, not days)
  • Health, reports & announcements
  • Support workspace (with consent, audited)

The services — the engines behind the apps

⚙️ Samithi service (one on each server)
Everything a society needs day to day: secure sign-in for members and staff, all record-keeping, notice publishing and notification sending, Puruka listings, photo storage. Each request is served strictly from that society's own database — societies never see each other.
🧭 Platform service (control room only)
Runs the fleet: the society directory that turns a join code into the right server, fully automated onboarding of new societies (database created, keys issued, registry updated), health monitoring of every server, and operator sign-in protected by two-step verification.

The data — one locked room per society

🗄️ Society databases (MySQL)
Every society's records live in a separate database with its own key — like separate locked filing rooms in the same building. A fault or mistake in one society can never touch another's records.
🔐 Maranadhara Samithi 🔐 Demo society 🔐 Society C…
📇 Platform records & registry
The master list of societies and servers, operator accounts, health history, and the audit log. Each server also keeps a small "address book" of the societies it hosts.
Built into every layer

🔒 Encryption everywhere. All traffic uses HTTPS. PINs are stored scrambled — no one can read them, not even us.

🧱 Isolation. One database per society, with its own credentials and keys.

📝 Accountability. Operator support access requires two-step sign-in and lands in a permanent audit log the society can see.

🔄 Always up to date. App improvements arrive automatically — over-the-air for the mobile app, an update feed for the desktop.

🌐 Two languages. Sinhala and English through every screen.

🛟 Safety nets. Daily backups, health monitoring, and a documented way back for every change.

Why it is built this way

  • Trust: societies handle members' life savings and bereavement funds — isolation and encryption are non-negotiable.
  • Growth without surgery: a new society is onboarded by the console in minutes; new servers join the fleet the same way.
  • Safe change: every feature is proven on the testbed before it touches the production server real societies rely on.
  • Simple for people: members need only a join code, their NIC and a PIN — no email, no passwords, no forms.
